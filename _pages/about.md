I study how human immunity and pathogen evolution interact to shape infectious disease dynamics, and how to translate that understanding into better surveillance and prediction tools.

My research combines mathematical modeling, statistical inference, and machine learning to address interconnected questions across three areas. First, **immune dynamics**: how do life-course infection histories and immune imprinting shape antibody landscapes, cross-reactivity, and susceptibility to reinfection? Second, **pathogen evolution**: how can we predict antigenic change from genomic and serological data, using tools like deep learning-based HAI titer prediction? Third, **methodological advances for surveillance**: how do we estimate transmission in real time when traditional case counts are unreliable, for instance, using population-level viral load (Ct) distributions?

I have developed open-source tools including [SeroMetrics](https://github.com/byyangyby/SeroMetrics) for serological analysis and a [transformer-based model](https://huggingface.co/spaces/yyf031/Influenza_A_HAI_Titer_Prediction) for influenza antigenic prediction. My COVID-19 travel control and importation risk analyses were cited in US CDC and New Zealand Government reports, and the Ct-based surveillance methods have been adopted by other teams for influenza monitoring.

I have published around 50 peer-reviewed papers with first/corresponding-author work in *Nature Microbiology*, *Nature Communications*, *eLife*, *The Lancet Regional Health*, and *PLoS Pathogens*, and have led multiple externally funded projects as independent PI. Before my current position at HKU, I completed postdoctoral training at the University of Florida with Derek Cummings.

**I am actively recruiting master's students, PhD students, and postdocs** to join my group at the University of Macau starting August 2025. I welcome applicants from epidemiology, public health, computer science, mathematics, statistics, and related fields. Please reach out via email if interested.

## software & tools

- **[SeroMetrics](https://github.com/byyangyby/SeroMetrics)**: R package for analyzing serological data and quantifying antibody landscapes
- **[Influenza HAI Prediction](https://huggingface.co/spaces/yyf031/Influenza_A_HAI_Titer_Prediction)**: Deep learning model for predicting hemagglutination inhibition titers from HA sequences
